Hi my name is Patricia Kisinger and I have some advice for you!

Don't Give Up

I made the mistake of stopping when I got frustrated with Responsive Web Development. My excuse was always "Tomorrow. I'll work on it tomorrow." I ended up not working on anything for almost 6 months. This was obviously a huge set back as I had forgotten a lot of what I'd already learned. I began again having to do several lessons over again. I felt more stuck and frustrated but I decided that instead of dwelling on what I couldn't make sense of I was just going to go ahead and figure it out again later. It's actually helped and I've been much more motivated to keep practicing instead of giving up.

Focus on one language at a time.

It's very easy to be overwhelmed in tech when you're completely new to it. Ask anyone what language you should learn and they'll easily rattle off 5-6. I didn't know the difference between any of them, which were front end or back end. For a while, I thought it was expected that I had to learn HTML, CSS, Javascript, Java, Sass, Python, Ruby on Rails, jQuery and Swift. I thought It would take me forever to get a career I wanted

I realize now that every tech position is different and requires a different set of languages. They all require the basic HTML, CSS and Javascript. Some don't need much more and some require knowledge of at least one back end language. It's a lot easier to figure out and learn once you just start and take it day by day.

Money is my motivator! I'm constantly checking job postings for jobs that I could soon be applying for and the salary I could soon be making.
