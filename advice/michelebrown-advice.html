Hi my name is Michele Brown and I have some advice for you!

Tenacity is your friend

I started this tech journey a little over a month ago. Other than knowing the the basics of web surfing and saving files, I KNEW NOTHING.

It's also worth mentioning that I'm a "Xennial", which is the awkwardly named subgeneration that falls in between Gen X and Millenial. Meaning, I had to be introduced to computers as a child. While not native to technology, I still can eventually figure some things out.

Maybe you're like me and you feel you're at a slight disadvantage due to age/having a non-tech background.

There will be times that you'll want to give up. When this happens, BACK AWAY FROM THE COMPUTER, and go outside. Or get some sleep. You'll come back with fresh eyes and a fresh perspective.

And remember, Google is your friend.

Embrace the Frustration

If you're like me, everything with coding/learning new concepts in tech comes, well, NOT easy. Somebody could spoon feed me what to do, but I find that sweating through the many many (MANY) missteps solidifies the information in my head. I figure out for myself the how and the why. Slowly. Painfully. But it gets done.

I tell myself this mantra, about every 5 seconds or so: "If being in the technology field were easy, everyone would be doing it and then it wouldn't be in demand nor would it pay well."

Because the knowledge is hard won, you'll get a rush of dopamine to the brain when you refresh your page and voila! The page did what you wanted it to! So rewarding!

Think of all the character you're building. I feel like there's a pun in there somewhere.
