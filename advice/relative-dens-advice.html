Hi my name is Robert and I have some advice for you!

Expect Confusion!

When you start programming your first web app you are doing something that you have never done before. It's a new challenge, a new opportunity to learn & grow. Take advantage of resources, the web, google, books, friends & fellow students. They've been there too, so don't dispair, the solution exists, if you invest the time & put your heart into it, it will get easier & more enjoyable, come more naturally, & become more rewarding.

Confusion should be expected therefore, but you shouldn't allow it to discourage you. Stay positive, it's a learning experience, it's a new language, think of it as a language. Like learning a new language, it will take time & practice, but it will come to you. It will start to come more natually. Not sure where to start? Try to break down the problem into smaller pieces, then take on one piece at a time. Smaller parts are easier to break down & solve.

Expect some successes!

Take joy in you're successes, even the little ones! It will encourage you & help motivate you to dig into the next problem. Keep reading the code, practice, practice, practice coding, it's worth the effort. There will always be new problems to solve, but as you gain familiarity with the basics, you'll learn how to break the problem apart to work on the pieces.

You see when you encounter a new programming language you'll find that they have many similarities. The syntax may have differences, but the basic pieces are the same. So dig in and practice with one, and the next one will be easier.
