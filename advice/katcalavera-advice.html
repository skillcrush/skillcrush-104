Here are the 4 most important things I've learned on my coding journey so far.

Check out the Skillcrush forums often...

...whether you have a problem or not! Leaning on peers and instructors for help is a fantastic way to get help with your specific problem if you haven't been able to find an answer to your issue by searching online.

But even when you don't have an active problem, it's good to visit the forums from time to time for a few reasons:

  • Helping other people troubleshoot their problems not only helps them out, but it helps you build your problem-solving and debugging skills!
  • Answering other people's questions is a great way to solidify your own comprehension of a topic, as reviewing what you know in a real-world context can help your brain transfer the concepts from short-term to long-term memory storage.
  • On top of all that, interacting in the group helps you build a community of supportive peers who can act as collaborators, sounding boards for new ideas, and motivation to keep advancing.

Spellcheck first.

Before you do go posting a question on the forums, double check your code and the source code to make sure everything in your version is spelled and formatted correctly. I catch 75% of my potential questions at this stage!

Don't lose your momentum.

Finding time to code can be really hard, but it's really important that you don't go more than a few days without working on your code, especially if you're in the middle of a project or learning a tricky new concept.

Picking up where you left off is way way harder if you don't have a fresh memory of what you were doing! Letting too much time pass between coding sessions is a surefire way to make yourself more frustrated and make your projects drag on, because you'll have to keep looking back at previous lessons.

Make it personal.

When you do have an extra few minutes to work on a project, use that time to play and explore what you've created. Try a new effect you found online, customize colors and layouts, and edit your copy so that it reflects your voice.

Taking the extra time to customize and experiment with your designs is a perfect way to practice the concepts you've just learned. It can inspire you to connect more deeply with a project, help you develop your own personal style, and give you something to be proud of!

Above all... Don't. Give. Up.

Have fun and happy coding!
