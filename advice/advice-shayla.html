Take it from me, kid...

Imagine yourself a month from now

Haven't there been SO many times in your life when you go through a big change and you look back on yourself from a month or a year ago and think, "Wow, she had no idea how much she would learn and grow in that time, but she did." I always wish I could go back to myself and say, "STOP WORRYING! It will all come together in the end, you'll see!"

So now sometimes I like to think about myself a month in the future, coming back to say those things to me. :)

Don't wait until you feel comfortable

No one is ever ready and there's never going to be a point where you think, "Yep, now I'm ready to go out into the world and pitch someone to be their web developer!" Not going to happen.

So get out there and do it. Going through the process of working with a client is a better crashcourse than anything you can pay for. You'll make mistakes, sure, but you can recover. Plus, it starts bringing in that sweet $.

No one knows what they're doing

I'm really lucky, because my partner is a software developer and he let me in on a little secret: NO ONE knows what they're doing.

The Internet is held together with scotch tape and chewing gum. Professional developers use hacky work-around fixes all the time, because sometimes that's the only (or the fastest) way to solve a problem.

No one is a wizard, we're all just muddling muggles.
