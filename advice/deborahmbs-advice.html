Hi my name is Deborah and I have some advice for you!

Just do it

I have been thinking of learning how to code for ages. I have read many books on the topic, gone through numerous online how to's and watched Youtube Tutorials. Unfortunately I could not decide on what language to start which lead me to reading more, but I actually never started to code myself.

The big mistake was just gathering information instead of using the gathered information. It is a more or less learning a new language by only studying the grammar. This way it stays gibberish and it will not stick.

Free vs Paid

There are many free ressources online. Some are good, otheres are not so good. Main thing there is a lot of information available, but what should you choose?

I highly disliked the lack of structure in the free online material, but I was hesitant about paying for a course. My solution was to do free trials, so I could get an overview of the course material and teaching style. Many of these payed courses have a really supportive online community which helps a lot when you get stuck.

My preference is go for a payed course and use free ressources to give you a different perspective on the current topic you are studying.

Before you move on

Once I actually started to practice, I was so eager to learn that I moved to quickly from HTML, to CSS, to Javascript...

I did the exercises I had to do, but I did not really know what I was doing. So I took a break and started all over again. However this time, I took my time to learn and enjoy the learning process instead of being focused on many destinations.
