Hi my name is Phoebe and I have some advice for you!

Practice practice practice!

I've been learning coding on and off for almost a year now. I started with free tutorials online like Codecademythen I took a HTML/CSS course at General Assembly, which gives me more knowledge on basic HTML/CSS. It was when I took a UX Design course from Bloc that further developed my coding skills. By the end of that bootcamp, I was comfortably coding my portfolio.

After completing my portfolio, I stopped practicing writing codes and I somewhat forgot how to code! So I went back to Codecademy and refreshed my memory.

So bottom line, is practice, practice and practice some more! I love writing codes, and there is still so much to learn and I think there will always be more to learn! Next step - Javascript!

Github is your best friend

When I was first introduced to Git, I was like OMG HELP what is this!! Opening Terminal on my computer? WHAT! I am not exaggerating! What is command line?

I was so scared to even do anything with it at first, I avoided using Git as much as I could but... how am I gonna share my work with mentor? Fast forward... Once you get used to git, it is like your best friend. with a few simple git add, git commit and git push, your local repo is on your remote repo where you can share with anyone and collaborate with them!

Don't ever be scared when you hit a wall. There's always a solution on Google, someone is/was in the same situation and share their experience and solution. That's the beauty of technology!
