Hi my name is Danny and I have some advice for you!

Don't be afraid to walk away!

Your brain can only retain so much information at a time. Trying to learn a programming language by spending hours and hours in a single sitting, reading, practicing, debugging, etc... will work against you. Limit your study time to smaller chunks. I find that spending no more than 2-3 hours a day, with 15 to 25 minute breaks every 45 to 60 minutes works for me.

I didn't always do this. When I first started re-learning how to code, I would try and code/learn for hours upon hours at a time. Taking breaks only to eat or use the bathoom (we only rent water). In time I realized that I wasn't learning as much as I thought I had been. I knew I was reading all these books and doing all this practice, but I wasn't really moving forward. I then realized that it was because I was overloading my brain, and thus not retaining nearly as much as I thought I would.

So, give yourself a break. Don't try so hard, and relax. You'll learn and retain more by being nicer to your brain.
