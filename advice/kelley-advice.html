Hi, my name is Kelley and I have some advice for you!

Don't Worry About Doubt!

When you start programming -- or before you even start -- you might feel some doubt. Are you going to be successful? Are you going to fail? I had these same thoughts when I first started.
Once I started learning programming skills, though, I wasn't as nervous -- the unknown was becoming a little bit more "known," so to speak.

My advice is, don't let doubt prevent you from trying something just because you don't know what the outcome will be. Not everything is going to make sense right away,
and you have to keep practicing in order for it make sense.

Fake It Until You Make It!

Embracing your doubt will push you to really think about what you're coding, because, well, you doubt that it will work, and your code will be stronger for it.
Look for every little error, test it until it works and ask for help when you need it.

Sometimes just pretending to have confidence will help you feel confident. Once you start understanding how coding works
and you remember all those little things that seemed impossible to remember, you'll actually start to feel confident.
