Hi! My name is Dave.
Here's my advice for new coders:

Don't Take Mistakes Personally

Do you remember how many times you fell down when you were learning to walk? A lot. And it hurt. That's probably why you don't remember it. You blocked it away in your unconscious as a traumatic experience!

Coding is easy compared to that!

Somewhere along the line we learned that mistakes were bad. Maybe frustrated teachers or over-achieving parents taught us to think mistakes were bad and they punished us for them.

But, mistakes are how we learn. So, psssst! Here's a really big secret:
Mistakes are okay, and everyone makes them! Even great programmers!

You're talking to a computer. It has infinite patience. So learn to have patience with yourself. It's not yelling at you; it's confused by something you told it to do. It's just less tolerant of logical ambiguity than you are.

Emulate Experienced Coders

The best coders have strategies for dealing with frustration. Talk with a teacher or student you admire or even listen to podcasts where developers are interviewed. Coding is mostly frustration punctuated by tiny bits of genius. Learn how others handle that.

Do Physical Stuff Too

Programming can be intensly cerebral. But you're a whole person, so get up often and remember to get physical exercise that balances your programming.

Try a standing desk. Sitting is the new smoking, and seated programming for hours on end can lead to all kinds of physical challenges. So learn to work from various positions.
