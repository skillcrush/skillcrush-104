Hi. My name is Cynthia and I have some advice for you!

Confusion is your friend!

Confusion is your brain trying to wrap itself around something it doesn't understand. Something it's never encountered before, or is encountering in a new and different way.

Confusion means you're challenging yourself. Confusion is a double-dog-dare-you. Confusion means you're alive and learning and kicking and clawing.

You are confused. Therefore you ARE

When you are nose-to-nose with a new technology, whether you're learning to write your first programming language or just trying to become besties with the latest-greatest framework, there's always an uncomfortable phase where you know that the code you are looking at is doing this THING but you don't understand HOW exactly it's happening.

This is a normal part of the process of learning a new technology. In fact, it's a critical part of the process of learning a new technology.

You see, when you encounter a new programming language part of your learning process includes training your eye, and the best way to do that is to read a good deal of code even when you don't totally understand what it's doing.

And gradually, through repetition, it all starts to make sense.

Remember when you learned to read, and one day it went from letters to words to sentences?

No?

Neither do I!

But we both know it happened.

So: Trust the process. And trust yourself.

And learn to love Confusion
