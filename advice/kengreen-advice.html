... and I was asked to pass along some advice for "newbies"

First of all, I have a problem with the term "newbie". I find it demeaning. I have been a software developer for over 28 years and I still feel like a "newbie" quite often. I try very hard to to keep the concept of Shosin in mind:

Shoshin (初心) is a concept in Zen Buddhism meaning "beginner's mind". It refers to having an attitude of openness, eagerness, and lack of preconceptions when studying a subject, even when studying at an advanced level, just as a beginner in that subject would. The term is especially used in the study of Zen Buddhism and Japanese martial arts.

Source: Wikipedia

In the beginner's mind there are many possibilities, in the expert's mind there are few.

"Shunryu Suzuki"

Off the top of my head, two of the most valuable pieces of advice I can give:

  • Learn how to program deliberately and not by coincidence
  • Read "Clean Code" by Robert C. Martin
