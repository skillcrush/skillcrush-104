Hi, my name is Jess

and these are a few things I wish I knew when I first started programming!

Don't fall down a rabbit hole

It's not possible to learn and retain everything there is to know about programming at once. When you're working on something it's easy to get hung up on unfamiliar terms and concepts and want to know everything about them. This often leads to following link after link about many other concepts, and before you know it you're researching something completely different!

When you're working on something, research and learn just enough to get your project done. Make a note of unfamiliar concepts you'd like to learn more about and look them up later when you have free time.

Stop doing tutorials

Tutorials are awesome and a great way to begin your coding journey, but unless you start working on your own projects you won't truly learn to program independently. Step away from the tutorials and work on something that interests you.

Just keep making things and eventually the stuff you found difficult at first will become second nature. Start small and don't overwhelm yourself.

Take breaks

Give your brain a chance to rest and you'll remember more than if you try to cram everything into a single learning session.
