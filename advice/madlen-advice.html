Hi there, thanks for stopping by!

I don't feel qualified to give anyone advice but I am happy to tell you what works for me. I spent some time browsing through the advice.hmtl files of previous skillcrush participants and I learned a thing or two from some of the 408 submissions (as of Jan 17th, 2017). Did I read all of them? No, I didn't but I am a data scientist so I couldn't resist analyzing this treasure trove of data that Skillcrush provided here.

Be curious

I wrote an R and a Python script (available on github) that provided the following insight:

  • 408 participants submitted the challenge
  • 7 (un)knowingly deleted the section tags that I used to analyze the text
  • A whoping 88% (353 out of 401) participants changed the text in the document (i.e., gave advice). That tells us that Skillcrushers are in general really engaged learners! It is possible that some of the 48 participants that did not alter the text still changed the styling (which I did not analyze).
  • A dog seems more than twice as important to learning how to code than a cat (dog mentioned 12, cat 6 times).

Just for fun, I created a wordcloud of the advice.html files that were altered by learners. In a wordcloud, the size of the word represents the frequency of the word in the analyzed data set (N = 353). The most common word was "learning", which was mentioned 1112 times. The outer (smallest) words had a frequency of two.
