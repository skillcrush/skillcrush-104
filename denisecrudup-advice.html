From Community College Instructor to Novice Coder

By Denise Crudup

You Must Fail to Succeed

I've learned more from figuring out why my code doesn't work than when it does. Backtracking and reviewing your code is the only way to learn.

Small Mistakes Can Have Major Consequences

No exaggeration; I've spent HOURS trying to figure out why an application wouldn't work. Sometimes is was a mispelling, other times I missed an important piece of punctuation.

Why Does Any of That Matter?

Computers are stupid! Although we (society) have ascribed human qualities to computers (i.e. comparing it to a human brain), computers are extraordinarily literal; they do not have the ability to make inferences and understand shades of gray. That's why when you type a comma rather than a period, it "reads" it as a catastrophic error. Unlike a human, it doesn't assume that it's a human error (a typo) but still understand what the author intended. Coding hones your attention to detail!

Embrace Frustration

Coding is not difficult, just frustrating (which is a good thing). Through this process, I've learned that coding is not a mystical process. With time and effort, everyone can learn to code.
